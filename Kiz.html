Introduction

Rhinos, an important species in our ecosystem is endangered. There are only five types of rhinos left in the world. People are killing all the innocent rhinos just for their horns because they believe the horn can be made to oriental medicine that would cure many diseases which is not true at all! Drinking the medicine from the rhino horn is just like biting your nails and hair, it is made out of the exact same material. Another reason is that they would get the horn to make it a symbol of peace and wealth, also to make dangers and accessories.

Rhinos have existed on earth for 4 thousand years now, but its population has decreased by 90% since 1970. Rhinos used to live in the wild in Vietnam, however they have disappeared due to extinction. There are only around 30 thousand rhinos left in the wild all over the world. Rhinos are very important, if they disappear, a huge part of the food chain will be missing which will affect the ecosystem.

Overall, the population of the rhinos is decreasing very fast and they may extinct in just a few decades. This means that the next generation would look at the rhinos only in pictures and only on the internet, they will not be able to see what a rhino look like in real life even if they wanted to! When they are poaching rhinos for its horn, with the pain, the rhino could die faster. One of the ways for the rhinos to protect themselves is by the horns which are now gone. How will they keep themselves safe in the wild with all the attacks? Another reason for their extinction is their habitats are changing because of all the cities human lives in, it is taking over all the spaces for animals to live.

Five Types of Rhinos

White Rhino

The White rhinos are one of the types that came back from extinction in the early 20th century. From 50-100 rhinos to around 20,000. The major of White rhinos live in Southern Africa.

Black Rhino

In 1970, there were about 70,000 Black rhinos. But just in 20 years after, their population decreased 96% leaving only 5,000 ~ 5,500 Black rhinos left.

Javan Rhino

The Javan rhinos has the least population out of all types. There are only around 67 Javan rhinos left and they are living in the Ujung Kulon National Park.

Indian Rhino (The Greater One-horned Rhino)

The Indian rhino also came back from extinction. Its population has increased to more than 3,500 since the late 20th century.

Sumatran Rhino (Asian Two-horned Rhino)

Another very rare type of rhino is the Sumatran rhinos. There are only about 80 left on earth. This is because of people killing it for the horns and also they are not used to their habitat changing.
